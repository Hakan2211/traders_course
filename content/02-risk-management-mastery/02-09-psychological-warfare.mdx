## LOSS AVERSION: THE DEADLIEST BIAS

Let's start with the <Highlight variant="yellow">cognitive bias</Highlight> that destroys more traders than all others combined:

**<Highlight variant="yellow">LOSS AVERSION</Highlight>** = The psychological phenomenon where losses feel approximately 2.5× more painful
than equivalent gains feel pleasurable

### <Highlight variant="accent">THE KAHNEMAN-TVERSKY EXPERIMENT</Highlight>

**<Highlight variant="yellow">Scenario A:</Highlight>**

→ You're given $100 for free<br/>
→ How do you feel?<br/>
→ Happiness level: +5/10<br/>

**<Highlight variant="yellow">Scenario B:</Highlight>**

→ You're given $100, then it's taken away<br/>
→ How do you feel?<br/>
→ Pain level: -12/10<br/>

**<Highlight variant="blue">Same net result ($0 change), but the emotional experience is VASTLY different.</Highlight>**

**<Highlight variant="red">Losing $100 hurts 2.5× more than gaining $100 feels good.</Highlight>**

### <Highlight variant="accent">LOSS AVERSION IN TRADING: THE DEATH SPIRAL</Highlight>

**<Highlight variant="yellow">PHASE 1: THE SETUP</Highlight>**

You enter a trade:

→ Long EUR/USD at 1.1000<br/>
→ Stop: 1.0950 (50 pips, 1% risk)<br/>
→ Target: 1.1100 (100 pips, 2% gain)<br/>

**<Highlight variant="blue">Emotional state: Neutral to slightly optimistic.</Highlight>**

**<Highlight variant="yellow">PHASE 2: THE DRAWDOWN</Highlight>**

Price moves against you:

→ EUR/USD drops to 1.0970 (30 pips against)<br/>
→ You're now -0.6% unrealized loss<br/>

**<Highlight variant="blue">Your logical brain:</Highlight>**

→ "This is normal. Price action is within expected range. My stop is at 1.0950. I'm fine."<br/>

**<Highlight variant="blue">Your emotional brain:</Highlight>**

→ "I'm losing! This feels terrible! The pain is building! I need this to stop!"<br/>

**<Highlight variant="red">Loss aversion is kicking in. The pain of this small loss is overwhelming.</Highlight>**

**<Highlight variant="yellow">PHASE 3: THE APPROACH</Highlight>**

Price continues falling:

→ EUR/USD at 1.0955 (45 pips against)<br/>
→ You're now -0.9% unrealized loss<br/>
→ Your stop is 5 pips away<br/>

**<Highlight variant="blue">Your logical brain:</Highlight>**

→ "My analysis is probably wrong. I should accept the stop."<br/>

**<Highlight variant="blue">Your emotional brain (SCREAMING):</Highlight>**

→ "NO! If you take the stop, the LOSS BECOMES REAL!"<br/>
→ "Right now it's just unrealized! It could come back!"<br/>
→ "You haven't ACTUALLY lost until you close it!"<br/>
→ "If you just move the stop a little further, it might reverse!"<br/>

**<Highlight variant="red">Loss aversion is now in full control.</Highlight>**

You make the fatal decision: MOVE THE STOP to 1.0900.

**<Highlight variant="yellow">PHASE 4: THE DISASTER</Highlight>**

Price blows through your original stop:

→ EUR/USD hits 1.0950 (your original stop)<br/>
→ Continues to 1.0930<br/>
→ Continues to 1.0900 (your new stop)<br/>

**You move it again to 1.0850.**

Price hits 1.0850.

Final loss: 150 pips = -3% (instead of planned -1%)

**<Highlight variant="yellow">PHASE 5: THE AFTERMATH</Highlight>**

You finally close at -3%.

**<Highlight variant="blue">Emotional devastation:</Highlight>**

→ Not only did you lose 3× more than planned<br/>
→ You violated your own rules<br/>
→ You feel weak, undisciplined, like a failure<br/>
→ Your confidence is shattered<br/>

**<Highlight variant="yellow">Next trade:</Highlight>**

→ You enter with doubt<br/>
→ You exit winners early (afraid they'll reverse)<br/>
→ You hold losers even longer (trying to avoid another painful loss)<br/>

**<Highlight variant="red">Loss aversion has infected your entire system.</Highlight>**

Within 2-3 weeks, your account is down -15%.

**Loss aversion didn't just cost you 2% extra on one trade. It destroyed your psychological capital and cascaded into account destruction.**

### <Highlight variant="accent">WHY LOSS AVERSION IS SO DEADLY</Highlight>

**<Highlight variant="blue">Loss aversion causes:</Highlight>**

**<X className="inline-block" stroke="#ef4444" />** Moving stops further away (turning -1R into -3R losses)<br/>
**<X className="inline-block" stroke="#ef4444" />** Holding losers too long ("It could come back!")<br/>
**<X className="inline-block" stroke="#ef4444" />** Cutting winners too early (afraid they'll reverse and become losses)<br/>
**<X className="inline-block" stroke="#ef4444" />** Averaging down (adding to losing positions to "lower the average")<br/>
**<X className="inline-block" stroke="#ef4444" />** Not using stops at all (because stops "lock in losses")<br/>
**<X className="inline-block" stroke="#ef4444" />** Emotional paralysis (unable to close a losing trade)<br/>

**Result: Small losses become large losses. Winning systems become losing systems.**

### <Highlight variant="accent">THE ANTIDOTE TO LOSS AVERSION</Highlight>

**<Highlight variant="yellow">REFRAME #1:</Highlight>** Losses are business expenses, not personal failures

**<Highlight variant="blue">You own a restaurant:</Highlight>**

→ Rent: $3,000/month (expected expense)<br/>
→ Food costs: $8,000/month (expected expense)<br/>
→ You don't emotionally agonize over these costs<br/>

**<Highlight variant="blue">Trading:</Highlight>**

→ Expected losses: 40-50% of trades (expected expense)<br/>
→ Cost of doing business: -0.5R average per losing trade<br/>
→ These are no different from restaurant expenses<br/>

**<Highlight variant="red">Losing trades are the COST of accessing winning trades.</Highlight>**<br/>

**<Highlight variant="yellow">REFRAME #2:</Highlight>** Unrealized losses are real losses

**<Highlight variant="blue">Your brain thinks:</Highlight>**

→ "It's not a loss until I close it!"<br/>

**<Highlight variant="blue">Reality:</Highlight>**

→ Your account equity ALREADY reflects the loss<br/>
→ You've ALREADY lost the money<br/>
→ Holding doesn't change this fact<br/>
→ Closing = accepting reality (rational)<br/>
→ Holding = denying reality (irrational)<br/>

**<Highlight variant="yellow">Ask yourself:</Highlight>** "If I had no position, would I enter this trade RIGHT NOW at this price?"

**If the answer is NO, close immediately.**

**<Highlight variant="yellow">REFRAME #3:</Highlight>** Pre-commit to losses before entry

**<Highlight variant="blue">Before entering:</Highlight>**

→ "I accept that this trade has a 50% chance of losing."<br/>
→ "I accept that I will lose $100 on this trade if my stop is hit."<br/>
→ "I have already mentally spent this $100 for the opportunity to make $200."

Sign this commitment in your journal before entry.

When the stop is hit, you're not losing $100. You're executing a pre-made agreement.

## OVERCONFIDENCE: THE WINNING STREAK KILLER

Loss aversion destroys traders during losing streaks.
But there's an equally deadly bias that strikes during winning streaks:

**<Highlight variant="red">OVERCONFIDENCE</Highlight>**

### <Highlight variant="accent">THE OVERCONFIDENCE PHENOMENON</Highlight>

**<Highlight variant="yellow">Study (1981, Svenson):</Highlight>** Researchers asked people to rate their driving ability.

**<Highlight variant="blue">Results:</Highlight>**

→ 93% rated themselves as "above average" drivers<br/>

**<Highlight variant="red">Mathematical impossibility:</Highlight>** By definition, only 50% can be above average.

**<Highlight variant="blue">Conclusion:</Highlight>** Humans massively overestimate their abilities.\*\*

### <Highlight variant="accent">OVERCONFIDENCE IN TRADING: THE PATTERN</Highlight>

**<Highlight variant="yellow">WEEK 1-4: THE LEARNING PHASE</Highlight>**

**<Highlight variant="blue">You're cautious:</Highlight>**

→ Risk 0.5-1% per trade<br/>
→ Follow rules religiously<br/>
→ Double-check every entry<br/>
→ Journal every trade<br/>

**<Highlight variant="blue">Result:</Highlight>** Small gains, building confidence.

**<Highlight variant="yellow">WEEK 5-8: THE WINNING STREAK</Highlight>**

**<Highlight variant="blue">You hit a hot streak:</Highlight>**

→ 7 wins out of 10 trades<br/>
→ Up +12R in 4 weeks<br/>
→ Everything you touch turns to gold<br/>

**<Highlight variant="blue">Your brain concludes:</Highlight>**

→ "I'm better than I thought!"<br/>
→ "I've figured it out!"<br/>
→ "My analysis is superior!"<br/>
→ "I have a special gift for reading markets!"<br/>

**<Highlight variant="blue">Reality:</Highlight>**

→ You hit a favorable market regime<br/>
→ Your system is working as expected (55% win rate)<br/>
→ Random variance gave you a positive cluster<br/>
→ Your skill hasn't changed<br/>

**<Highlight variant="yellow">WEEK 9: THE OVERCONFIDENCE TRADE</Highlight>**

**<Highlight variant="blue">A setup appears:</Highlight>**

→ It's a B-grade setup (normally you'd skip)<br/>
→ But you're on a hot streak<br/>
→ You FEEL like you can't lose<br/>

**<Highlight variant="blue">Overconfidence manifests:</Highlight>**

→ "My win rate is 70% lately, I'll increase to 2% risk"<br/>
→ "This is going to work, I just know it"<br/>
→ "I'll take this marginal setup—I've earned the right"<br/>

**<Highlight variant="red">You violate your rules:</Highlight>**

→ Enter with 2% risk (double your normal)<br/>
→ Take a B-grade setup (should be A-grade only)<br/>
→ Skip your pre-trade checklist (you "just know")<br/>

**<Highlight variant="blue">The inevitable outcome:</Highlight>**

**<Highlight variant="red">The trade fails:</Highlight>**

→ Loses -2% (double damage)<br/>
→ Shakes your confidence<br/>
→ "How did this happen? I was doing so well!"<br/>

**<Highlight variant="blue">But worse:</Highlight>**

→ You take the next trade with 2% risk (trying to recover)<br/>
→ That loses too<br/>
→ Now you're down -4% in two trades<br/>

The winning streak that built confidence becomes the setup for overconfidence that destroys it.

### <Highlight variant="accent">WHY OVERCONFIDENCE IS DEADLY</Highlight>

**<Highlight variant="blue">Overconfidence causes:</Highlight>**

**<X className="inline-block" stroke="#ef4444" />** Increasing position size after wins (risking more when you "feel hot")<br/>
**<X className="inline-block" stroke="#ef4444" />** Taking lower-quality setups (B/C grades instead of A grades)<br/>
**<X className="inline-block" stroke="#ef4444" />** Skipping risk checks ("I don't need to check, I know it's good")<br/>
**<X className="inline-block" stroke="#ef4444" />** Abandoning rules ("Rules are for beginners, I understand markets now")<br/>
**<X className="inline-block" stroke="#ef4444" />** Overtrading (taking too many positions, exceeding portfolio heat)<br/>
**<X className="inline-block" stroke="#ef4444" />** Ignoring regime changes ("My system works in all conditions")<br/>

**<Highlight variant="blue">Result:</Highlight>** The best winning streaks become the setup for the worst losing streaks.

### <Highlight variant="accent">THE ANTIDOTE TO OVERCONFIDENCE</Highlight>

**<Highlight variant="yellow">DISCIPLINE #1:</Highlight>** Fixed risk regardless of recent results

**<Highlight variant="blue">Create this IRON RULE:</Highlight>**

→ "I risk 1% per trade."<br/>
→ "This percentage NEVER changes based on recent wins or losses."<br/>
→ "My recent performance has ZERO influence on my next position size."<br/>

After 5 wins: Still 1%<br/>
After 10 wins: Still 1%<br/>
→ After your best month ever: Still 1%<br/>

**<Highlight variant="yellow">DISCIPLINE #2:</Highlight>** Track your edge statistically, not emotionally

**<Highlight variant="blue">After a winning streak, don't think:</Highlight>**

**<X className="inline-block" stroke="#ef4444" />** "I'm amazing! I've got the magic touch!"<br/>

**<Highlight variant="blue">Instead, think:</Highlight>**

**<CheckCircle className="inline-block" stroke="#22c55e" />** "I'm 7W-3L over 10 trades = 70% win rate"<br/>
**<CheckCircle className="inline-block" stroke="#22c55e" />** "My historical win rate is 55%"<br/>
**<CheckCircle className="inline-block" stroke="#22c55e" />** "I'm currently 15% above my expected win rate"<br/>
**<CheckCircle className="inline-block" stroke="#22c55e" />** "This is variance, not skill improvement"<br/>
**<CheckCircle className="inline-block" stroke="#22c55e" />** "Regression to the mean is coming"<br/>

**<Highlight variant="yellow">DISCIPLINE #3:</Highlight>** Increase scrutiny during winning streaks

**<Highlight variant="blue">Counter-intuitive but critical:</Highlight>**

**<Highlight variant="red">When you're winning, CHECK YOURSELF MORE, not less:</Highlight>**

→ Double-check every setup grade<br/>
→ Verify risk calculations manually<br/>
→ Review rules before each trade<br/>
→ Journal MORE during wins (to catch overconfidence early)<br/>

**Winning streaks are WHEN you're most vulnerable to overconfidence-driven mistakes.**

**<Highlight variant="yellow">DISCIPLINE #4:</Highlight>** The "Remind Me I'm Ordinary" protocol

**<Highlight variant="blue">After big wins, read this aloud:</Highlight>**

→ "I did not become a better trader in the last week."<br/>
→ "My edge has not changed."<br/>
→ "Favorable variance gave me this result."<br/>
→ "If I change my behavior because I'm winning, I will lose."<br/>
→ "Discipline during wins determines if I survive the coming losses."<br/>

## THE SUNK COST FALLACY: AVERAGING DOWN INTO OBLIVION

Another deadly cognitive bias that destroys traders:

**<Highlight variant="red">SUNK COST FALLACY</Highlight>** = Continuing to invest in something because you've already invested, regardless of current logic

### <Highlight variant="accent">THE CLASSIC SUNK COST EXAMPLE</Highlight>

**<Highlight variant="blue">Scenario:</Highlight>**

You bought a $100 concert ticket two months ago.

Concert day arrives. You're sick with the flu. Going to the concert will make you worse. You'll be miserable.

**<Highlight variant="blue">Logical decision:</Highlight>** Stay home, rest, recover.

**<Highlight variant="blue">Sunk cost brain:</Highlight>**

→ "But I already paid $100!"<br/>
→ "If I don't go, I wasted the money!"<br/>
→ "I have to go to make it worth it!"<br/>

You go. You're miserable. You get worse. You miss work for three days.

The $100 was already gone the moment you bought the ticket. Going to the concert didn't save it—it just added suffering.

### <Highlight variant="accent">SUNK COST IN TRADING: AVERAGING DOWN</Highlight>

**<Highlight variant="yellow">PHASE 1: THE INITIAL POSITION</Highlight>**

**<Highlight variant="blue">You go long EUR/USD:</Highlight>**

→ Entry: 1.1000<br/>
→ Stop: 1.0950<br/>
→ Size: 0.1 lots<br/>
→ Risk: $100<br/>

**<Highlight variant="yellow">PHASE 2: THE DRAWDOWN</Highlight>**

EUR/USD drops to 1.0970 (-30 pips).

**<Highlight variant="blue">Sunk cost brain activates:</Highlight>**

→ "I'm already down $60"<br/>
→ "If I add another position at 1.0970, my average entry becomes 1.0985"<br/>
→ "Then I only need price to get to 1.0985 to break even, not all the way to 1.1000!"<br/>
→ "This is smart—I'm lowering my breakeven point!"<br/>

You add 0.1 lots at 1.0970.

**<Highlight variant="blue">New situation:</Highlight>**

→ Position 1: 0.1 lots at 1.1000<br/>
→ Position 2: 0.1 lots at 1.0970<br/>
→ Total: 0.2 lots, average entry 1.0985<br/>
→ Total risk: $200 (doubled)<br/>

**<Highlight variant="yellow">PHASE 3: THE CONTINUED DROP</Highlight>**

EUR/USD drops to 1.0940 (-60 pips from original).

**<Highlight variant="blue">Sunk cost brain (now desperate):</Highlight>**

→ "I'm already down $120 on position 1 and $60 on position 2 = $180 total"<br/>
→ "If I add at 1.0940, my average becomes 1.0970"<br/>
→ "I can break even much faster!"<br/>

You add 0.1 lots at 1.0940.

**<Highlight variant="blue">New situation:</Highlight>**

→ Total: 0.3 lots, average entry 1.0970<br/>
→ Total risk: $300 (tripled)<br/>

**<Highlight variant="yellow">PHASE 4: THE DISASTER</Highlight>**

EUR/USD breaks through 1.0920.

Your original stop at 1.0950 is long gone.

You have 0.3 lots with unrealized loss of $270.

**<Highlight variant="blue">Options:</Highlight>**

→ Close now: Lose $270 (2.7% of account)<br/>
→ Average down again at 1.0920 (0.4 lots total, $360 at risk)<br/>
→ Hold and pray<br/>

**<Highlight variant="blue">You hold.</Highlight>**

EUR/USD crashes to 1.0850 due to unexpected economic data.

Final loss: $450 (4.5% of account)

What was supposed to be a -1% loss ($100) became a -4.5% loss because of averaging down.

### <Highlight variant="accent">WHY AVERAGING DOWN IS DEADLY</Highlight>

**<Highlight variant="blue">The illusion:</Highlight>**

→ "Lowering my average entry price is smart math!"<br/>

**<Highlight variant="blue">The reality:</Highlight>**

Your original entry was WRONG<br/>
→ Averaging down is doubling down on a wrong thesis<br/>
→ You're massively increasing exposure to a losing position<br/>
→ If you're wrong (and you are—you're losing), you're multiplying the damage<br/>

**<Highlight variant="blue">Averaging down transforms:</Highlight>**

**<CheckCircle className="inline-block" stroke="#22c55e" />** A manageable -1R loss<br/>
**<X className="inline-block" stroke="#ef4444" />** Into a catastrophic -3R to -5R loss

### <Highlight variant="accent">THE ANTIDOTE TO SUNK COST FALLACY</Highlight>

**<Highlight variant="yellow">RULE #1:</Highlight>** Never add to a losing position

**<Highlight variant="blue">EVER.</Highlight>**

→ Not to "lower your average."<br/>
→ Not because "it's an even better price now."<br/>
→ Not because "it HAS to bounce from here."

If your analysis was right, you wouldn't be losing.

Adding to a loser is admitting you were wrong but refusing to accept the consequence.

**<Highlight variant="yellow">RULE #2:</Highlight>** Each trade is independent

**<Highlight variant="blue">Mental reframe:</Highlight>**

**<Highlight variant="blue">Instead of thinking:</Highlight>**

**<X className="inline-block" stroke="#ef4444" />** "I'm already down $60 on this position, so..."<br/>

**<Highlight variant="blue">Think:</Highlight>**

**<CheckCircle className="inline-block" stroke="#22c55e" />** "Forget what I'm already down. If I had NO position right now, would I enter at this price?"<br/>
If the answer is NO, close immediately. Don't add.

**<Highlight variant="yellow">RULE #3:</Highlight>** Adding is ONLY for winners

**<Highlight variant="blue">The professional approach:</Highlight>**

**<X className="inline-block" stroke="#ef4444" />** NEVER add to losers (averaging down).<br/>
**<CheckCircle className="inline-block" stroke="#22c55e" />** ALWAYS consider adding to winners (pyramiding).

**<Highlight variant="blue">Example:</Highlight>**

Long EUR/USD at 1.1000 → Price goes to 1.1050 (+50 pips) → Add 0.05 lots at 1.1050

**<Highlight variant="blue">Why this works:</Highlight>**

→ You're adding to a position that's PROVEN your analysis correct<br/>
→ Your average entry worsens (1.1017), but you're in profit<br/>
→ If it reverses, your first position still profits

**This is the opposite of averaging down—and it's what professionals do.**

## REVENGE TRADING: RAGE AGAINST THE MARKET

Perhaps the most emotionally destructive pattern:

**<Highlight variant="red">REVENGE TRADING</Highlight> = Trading with the primary motivation of "getting back" at the market for a previous loss**

## COGNITIVE BIASES: THE HIDDEN SABOTEURS

Beyond the big three (loss aversion, overconfidence, sunk cost), dozens of cognitive biases sabotage your trading:

### <Highlight variant="accent">BIAS #1: ANCHORING</Highlight>

**<Highlight variant="blue">Definition:</Highlight>** Over-relying on the first piece of information you receive.

**<Highlight variant="blue">In trading:</Highlight>**

You buy AAPL at $180.

Price drops to $170.

**<Highlight variant="blue">Anchoring bias:</Highlight>**

→ Your brain is anchored to $180 as "fair value"<br/>
→ $170 feels "cheap" relative to your entry<br/>
→ "It HAS to go back to $180, that's where I bought it!"<br/>

**<Highlight variant="blue">Reality:</Highlight>**

→ The market doesn't care where you bought it<br/>
→ $180 is irrelevant; only current price action matters<br/>
→ Your anchor is creating irrational holding behavior<br/>

**<Highlight variant="green">Antidote:</Highlight>** Ask yourself, "If I had no position, would I buy at this price?"<br/>

### <Highlight variant="accent">BIAS #2: CONFIRMATION BIAS</Highlight>

**<Highlight variant="blue">Definition:</Highlight>** Seeking information that confirms your existing belief while ignoring contradictory evidence.

**<Highlight variant="blue">In trading:</Highlight>**

You're bullish on EUR/USD (you're long).

**<Highlight variant="blue">Confirmation bias:</Highlight>**

→ You see bullish chart patterns everywhere<br/>
→ You focus on bullish news<br/>
→ You dismiss bearish indicators as "noise"<br/>
→ You interpret every price move as "about to rally"<br/>

**<Highlight variant="blue">Reality:</Highlight>**

→ Price is making lower lows (bearish)<br/>
→ Your bias is blinding you to reality<br/>
→ You're ignoring your stop signal<br/>

**<Highlight variant="green">Antidote:</Highlight>** Actively seek evidence AGAINST your position. "What would invalidate my thesis?"<br/>

### <Highlight variant="accent">BIAS #3: RECENCY BIAS</Highlight>

**<Highlight variant="blue">Definition:</Highlight>** Overweighting recent events and assuming they'll continue.

**<Highlight variant="blue">In trading:</Highlight>**

**<Highlight variant="yellow">Scenario A:</Highlight>**

→ You've won 5 trades in a row<br/>
→ Recency bias: "I'm on fire! Everything I touch wins! I should take every setup I see!"<br/>
→ Result: Overtrading, overconfidence, eventual crash<br/>

**<Highlight variant="yellow">Scenario B:</Highlight>**

→ You've lost 5 trades in a row<br/>
→ Recency bias: "My system is broken. Nothing works anymore. I should stop trading."<br/>
→ Result: Abandon profitable system during normal variance<br/>

**<Highlight variant="green">Antidote:</Highlight>** Track performance over 100+ trades, not last 5. "What's my expectancy over 100 trades?"<br/>

### <Highlight variant="accent">BIAS #4: HINDSIGHT BIAS</Highlight>

**<Highlight variant="blue">Definition:</Highlight>** After an event, believing you "knew it all along."

**<Highlight variant="blue">In trading:</Highlight>**

You missed a big move in Gold:

→ "I KNEW Gold was going to rally! I should have taken that trade!"<br/>

**<Highlight variant="blue">Reality:</Highlight>**

→ At the time, the setup was unclear<br/>  
→ Your rules said to skip it (correctly)<br/>
→ You're reconstructing memory to feel prescient<br/>

**<Highlight variant="red">Danger:</Highlight>**

→ Next time a similar setup appears, you violate rules "because I knew it last time"<br/>
→ You take bad trades based on hindsight, not real-time analysis<br/>

**<Highlight variant="green">Antidote:</Highlight>** Journal your thinking BEFORE trades. Refer to contemporaneous notes, not memory.<br/>

### <Highlight variant="accent">BIAS #5: GAMBLER'S FALLACY</Highlight>

**<Highlight variant="blue">Definition:</Highlight>** Believing that past random events affect future probabilities.

**<Highlight variant="blue">In trading:</Highlight>**

**<Highlight variant="yellow">Scenario:</Highlight>**

→ You've lost 5 trades in a row<br/>
→ Gambler's fallacy: "I'm DUE for a win! The next trade HAS to be a winner!"<br/>

**<Highlight variant="blue">Reality:</Highlight>**

→ Each trade is independent<br/>
→ Past losses don't increase probability of next win<br/>
→ Your system has a 50% win rate — you could lose 10 in a row<br/>

**<Highlight variant="red">Danger:</Highlight>**

→ Increasing position size "because you're due"<br/>
→ Taking marginal setups "because the odds are in your favor now"<br/>

**<Highlight variant="green">Antidote:</Highlight>** Understand independence. Every trade has the same probability regardless of recent results.<br/>
