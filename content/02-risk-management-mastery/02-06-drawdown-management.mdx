## WHAT IS DRAWDOWN? (THE DEFINITION THAT HAUNTS)

Let's start with the precise definition:

**<Highlight variant="blue">DRAWDOWN</Highlight> = The decline from a peak to a trough in your account equity**

More specifically:

<Math>
  {
    '\\text{Drawdown } \\% = \\frac{\\text{Peak Equity} - \\text{Current Equity}}{\\text{Peak Equity}} \\times 100'
  }
</Math>
<br />

### <Highlight variant="accent">EXAMPLE 1: SIMPLE DRAWDOWN</Highlight>

**<Highlight variant="blue">Your account equity:</Highlight>**

→ Peak: $10,000<br/>
→ Current: $8,500<br/>

**<Highlight variant="blue">Calculate drawdown:</Highlight>**

<Math>
  {'\\begin{aligned}' +
    '\\text{Drawdown } \\% = \\frac{\\$10,000 - \\$8,500}{\\$10,000} \\times 100 \\\\' +
    '\\text{Drawdown } \\% = \\frac{\\$1,500}{\\$10,000} \\times 100 \\\\' +
    '\\text{Drawdown } \\% = 15\\%' +
    '\\end{aligned}'}

</Math>
<br />

**<Highlight variant="blue">Result:</Highlight>** You're in a 15% drawdown.

**<Highlight variant="blue">Translation:</Highlight>** You need an 17.6% gain just to get back to your peak.

### <Highlight variant="accent">EXAMPLE 2: DRAWDOWN AFTER GROWTH</Highlight>

**<Highlight variant="blue">Your account journey:</Highlight>**

→ Starting capital: $10,000<br/>
→ Grew to peak: $15,000 (+50%)<br/>
→ Fell to current: $12,000<br/>

**<Highlight variant="blue">Calculate drawdown:</Highlight>**

<Math>
  {'\\begin{aligned}' +
    '\\text{Drawdown } \\% = \\frac{\\$15,000 - \\$12,000}{\\$15,000} \\times 100 \\\\' +
    '\\text{Drawdown } \\% = \\frac{\\$3,000}{\\$15,000} \\times 100 \\\\' +
    '\\text{Drawdown } \\% = 20\\%' +
    '\\end{aligned}'}
</Math>
<br />

You're in a 20% drawdown from peak.

**<Highlight variant="blue">Important distinction:</Highlight>**

→ <Highlight variant="yellow">You're still up +20%</Highlight> from your original $10,000<br/>
→ <Highlight variant="yellow">You're down -20%</Highlight> from your peak of $15,000<br/>
→ <Highlight variant="yellow">Psychologically, you feel like you lost</Highlight>, even though you're technically winning<br/>

**This is why drawdowns are so painful — they <Highlight variant="red">erase gains you already celebrated</Highlight>.**

## TYPES OF DRAWDOWN

### <Highlight variant="accent">1\. ABSOLUTE DRAWDOWN</Highlight>

→ Decline from your initial capital<br/>
→ <Highlight variant="blue">Example:</Highlight> Started with $10,000, now at $8,500 = -15% absolute drawdown<br/>
→ This is what beginners focus on<br/>

### <Highlight variant="accent">2\. RELATIVE DRAWDOWN (PEAK-TO-TROUGH)</Highlight>

→ Decline from your highest account value<br/>
→ <Highlight variant="blue">Example:</Highlight> Peak was $15,000, now at $12,000 = -20% relative drawdown<br/>
→ This is what professionals track<br/>

### <Highlight variant="accent">3\. MAXIMUM DRAWDOWN (MDD)</Highlight>

→ The largest peak-to-trough decline in your trading history<br/>
→ <Highlight variant="blue">Example:</Highlight> Your worst drawdown was -28% (happened once over 2 years)<br/>
→ This is what determines if your system is tradeable<br/>

**Professionals always measure relative drawdown from peak, because that's what determines
psychological damage and recovery difficulty.**

**<Highlight variant="yellow">Maximum Drawdown vs. Average Drawdown</Highlight>**

<Alert variant="info">
  <AlertDescription>
    <strong>Understanding both types of drawdown is critical.</strong><br /><br />

    <strong>Maximum Drawdown (MDD)</strong><br />
    <Highlight variant="yellow">Definition:</Highlight> The worst peak-to-trough decline in your entire trading history.<br />
    <Highlight variant="yellow">Example:</Highlight> Over 5 years of trading, your worst drawdown was -22%. That's your MDD.<br />
    <Highlight variant="yellow">Why it matters:</Highlight> MDD tells you the worst-case scenario your system has produced. It's your "stress test" result.<br />
    <Highlight variant="yellow">Professional benchmarks:</Highlight><br />
    → Excellent: MDD &lt; 10%<br />
    → Good: MDD 10-15%<br />
    → Acceptable: MDD 15-25%<br />
    → Concerning: MDD 25-35%<br />
    → Dangerous: MDD &gt; 35%<br /><br />

    <strong>Average Drawdown</strong><br />
    <Highlight variant="yellow">Definition:</Highlight> The average of all drawdown periods.<br />
    <Highlight variant="yellow">Example:</Highlight> Over 50 trades, you experienced 8 separate drawdown periods averaging -7.3%. That's your average drawdown.<br />
    <Highlight variant="yellow">Why it matters:</Highlight> Average drawdown tells you what "normal pain" looks like in your system. It's what you should psychologically expect regularly.<br /><br />

    <strong>The Relationship</strong><br />
    <Highlight variant="yellow">Typical pattern:</Highlight><br />
    → Average drawdown: -8%<br />
    → Maximum drawdown: -18%<br />
    <Highlight variant="yellow">Why it matters:</Highlight> Your MDD is usually 1.5× to 3× your average drawdown because occasionally losing streaks compound, market conditions turn against your strategy, bad luck clusters, and multiple negative factors align.<br /><br />

    <strong>Planning for MDD</strong><br />
    <Highlight variant="yellow">Rule of thumb:</Highlight> Assume your future MDD will be 1.5× your historical MDD.<br />
    <Highlight variant="yellow">Example:</Highlight> Your worst drawdown so far is -20%. Assume you'll eventually experience -30%.<br />
    <Highlight variant="yellow">Why?</Highlight><br />
    → Your sample size is limited<br />
    → Market conditions change<br />
    → Black swans happen<br />
    → Unlucky streaks compound<br />
    If you can't psychologically or financially survive 1.5× your current MDD, you're over-leveraged.

  </AlertDescription>
</Alert>

## MAXIMUM DRAWDOWN: THE NUMBER THAT DETERMINES SURVIVABILITY

Every trading system, every trader, every strategy has a Maximum Drawdown (MDD).

MDD = The largest peak-to-trough decline your system will experience

This number determines whether your system is:

**<CheckCircle className="w-4 h-4 inline" stroke="#22c55e" />** Tradeable (you can psychologically survive it)<br/>
**<AlertTriangle className="w-4 h-4 inline" stroke="#f59e0b" />** Marginal (requires exceptional discipline)<br/>
**<XCircle className="w-4 h-4 inline" stroke="#ef4444" />** Untradeable (will destroy you psychologically)<br/>

**<Highlight variant="blue">Important notes:</Highlight>**

**<Highlight variant="yellow">1. "Acceptable MDD" </Highlight>** is what you PLAN for<br/>
**<Highlight variant="yellow">2. "Realistic MDD"</Highlight>** is what ACTUALLY happens (Murphy's Law)<br/>
**<Highlight variant="blue">3. Always design risk management</Highlight>** for realistic MDD, not acceptable MDD<br/>

**<Highlight variant="blue">Example:</Highlight>**

You think you can handle a -15% drawdown. Your system historically produces -15% average drawdowns.

**<Highlight variant="red">Problem:</Highlight>** Your MAXIMUM drawdown will likely be 2-3× your average drawdown.

If average is -15%, maximum could be -30% to -45%.

**<Highlight variant="red">Can you survive -45%?</Highlight>**

If no, your system is untradeable for you—regardless of how profitable it is on paper.

### <Highlight variant="accent">CALCULATING YOUR EXPECTED MDD</Highlight>

**<Highlight variant="yellow">Method 1: Historical Analysis</Highlight>**

Run your system through historical data (backtest) for 1,000+ trades.

**<Highlight variant="blue">Record the worst drawdown:</Highlight>** That's your historical MDD.

**<Highlight variant="blue"> Multiply by 1.5-2.0:</Highlight>** That's your expected future MDD.

**<Highlight variant="blue">Example:</Highlight>**

→ Historical MDD: -22%<br/>
→ Expected future MDD: -33% to -44%<br/>
→ Design your risk management to survive -45% drawdown

**<Highlight variant="yellow">Method 2: Monte Carlo Simulation</Highlight>**

Take your system's statistics (win rate, avg win, avg loss, expectancy).

**<Highlight variant="blue">Run 10,000 simulated trading sequences.</Highlight>**

**<Highlight variant="blue">Find the worst drawdown across all simulations:</Highlight>** That's your likely MDD.

**<Highlight variant="blue">Example results:</Highlight>**

→ Average drawdown: -12%<br/>
→ 95th percentile drawdown: -28%<br/>
→ Worst drawdown (99th percentile): -41%<br/>

**<Highlight variant="yellow">Conclusion:</Highlight>** Your system could produce a -41% drawdown. Can you survive that?

### <Highlight variant="accent">THE MDD QUESTION YOU MUST ANSWER</Highlight>

**Before you trade your system live, ask yourself:**

**"If my account drops 40% from peak, will I continue trading this system with discipline, or will I:**

**<XCircle className="w-4 h-4 inline" stroke="#ef4444" />** Abandon the system?<br/>
**<XCircle className="w-4 h-4 inline" stroke="#ef4444" />** Start revenge trading?<br/>
**<XCircle className="w-4 h-4 inline" stroke="#ef4444" />** Increase risk to recover faster?<br/>
**<XCircle className="w-4 h-4 inline" stroke="#ef4444" />** Freeze and stop trading entirely?\*\*

**If you can't honestly answer "I'll continue with discipline," your position sizing is too large.**

**<Highlight variant="blue">The fix:</Highlight>** Reduce position size until your expected MDD is psychologically survivable.

→ If 1% risk per trade creates -40% MDD → Try 0.5% risk per trade (creates ~-20% MDD)<br/>
→ If 0.5% risk creates -20% MDD and that's survivable → You've found your optimal size<br/>

**Professional traders <Highlight variant="blue">size positions</Highlight> not for maximum return, but for <Highlight variant="yellow">SURVIVABLE MAXIMUM DRAWDOWN.</Highlight>**

## LOSING STREAK PROBABILITIES: THE MATH THAT HUMBLES

Let's address the psychological horror that haunts every trader:

"How many losses in a row can I expect?"

The answer is more than you think, and it will happen more often than you think.

### <Highlight variant="accent">THE LOSING STREAK FORMULA</Highlight>

<Math>
  {'\\begin{aligned}' +
    '\\text{Probability of N consecutive losses} = (\\text{Loss Rate}) ^ N \\\\' +
    '\\end{aligned}'}
</Math>
<br />

**<Highlight variant="blue">Example:</Highlight> 50% win rate system**

<Math>
  {'\\begin{aligned}' +
    '\\text{Probability of 5 losses in a row} = 0.50 ^ 5 = 0.03125 = 3.125\\%' +
    '\\end{aligned}'}
</Math>

**<Highlight variant="yellow">Translation:</Highlight>** Over 100 trades, you'll likely experience at least THREE separate 5-loss streaks.

## CIRCUIT BREAKERS: DAILY, WEEKLY, AND MONTHLY LOSS LIMITS

Professional traders don't just track maximum drawdown — they implement circuit breakers to prevent
small losses from becoming catastrophic.

**<Highlight variant="blue">Circuit breakers</Highlight>** = Pre-defined loss limits that trigger immediate trading cessation

### <Highlight variant="accent">THE THREE-TIER CIRCUIT BREAKER SYSTEM</Highlight>

**<Highlight variant="yellow">TIER 1: DAILY LOSS LIMIT</Highlight>**

**<Highlight variant="blue">Rule:</Highlight>** If you lose X% in a single day, stop trading for the day.

**<Highlight variant="blue">Recommended daily loss limits:</Highlight>**

→ Conservative: -2% to -3%<br/>
→ Moderate: -3% to -5%<br/>
→ Aggressive: -5% to -7%

**<Highlight variant="blue">Example:</Highlight>**

→ Your account: $10,000<br/>
→ Daily loss limit: -3% = $300<br/>
→ You lose -1.5% on Trade 1, -1.8% on Trade 2<br/>
→ Total: -3.3%<br/>
→ Circuit breaker triggered: STOP TRADING FOR THE DAY<br/>

**<Highlight variant="blue">Why this works:</Highlight>**

**<CheckCircle className="w-4 h-4 inline" stroke="#22c55e" />** Prevents emotional spiral (revenge trading after multiple losses)<br/>
**<CheckCircle className="w-4 h-4 inline" stroke="#22c55e" />** Limits damage from "bad days" (when nothing works)<br/>
**<CheckCircle className="w-4 h-4 inline" stroke="#22c55e" />** Forces you to step back and analyze<br/>
**<CheckCircle className="w-4 h-4 inline" stroke="#22c55e" />** Prevents a bad day from becoming a bad week<br/>

**<Highlight variant="yellow">TIER 2: WEEKLY LOSS LIMIT</Highlight>**

**<Highlight variant="blue">Rule:</Highlight>** If you lose X% in a single week, stop trading for the week.

**<Highlight variant="blue">Recommended weekly loss limits:</Highlight>**

→ Conservative: -5% to -7%<br/>
→ Moderate: -7% to -10%<br/>
→ Aggressive: -10% to -15%

**<Highlight variant="blue">Example:</Highlight>**

→ Your account: $10,000<br/>
→ Weekly loss limit: -7% = $700<br/>
→ Monday: -$150<br/>
→ Tuesday: -$200<br/>
→ Wednesday: -$180<br/>
→ Thursday: -$220<br/>
→ **<Highlight variant="blue">Total:</Highlight> -$750 = -7.5%**<br/>
→ **<Highlight variant="blue">Circuit breaker triggered:</Highlight> STOP TRADING FOR THE WEEK**<br/>

**<Highlight variant="blue">Why this works:</Highlight>**

**<CheckCircle className="w-4 h-4 inline" stroke="#22c55e" />** Prevents bad weeks from becoming catastrophic<br/>
**<CheckCircle className="w-4 h-4 inline" stroke="#22c55e" />** Gives you time to analyze what's wrong<br/>
**<CheckCircle className="w-4 h-4 inline" stroke="#22c55e" />** Protects against prolonged unfavorable market conditions<br/>
**<CheckCircle className="w-4 h-4 inline" stroke="#22c55e" />** Maintains psychological resilience<br/>

**<Highlight variant="yellow">TIER 3: MONTHLY LOSS LIMIT</Highlight>**

**<Highlight variant="blue">Rule:</Highlight>** If you lose X% in a month, stop trading or reduce size dramatically.

**<Highlight variant="blue">Recommended monthly loss limits:</Highlight>**

→ Conservative: -10%<br/>
→ Moderate: -12% to -15%<br/>
→ Aggressive: -15% to -20%

**Example:**

→ Your account: $10,000<br/>
→ Monthly loss limit: -12% = $1,200<br/>
→ Week 1: -$400<br/>
→ Week 2: -$300<br/>
→ Week 3: -$250<br/>
→ Week 4: -$350<br/>
→ **<Highlight variant="blue">Total:</Highlight>** -$1,300 = -13%<br/>
→ **<Highlight variant="blue">Circuit breaker triggered:</Highlight>** STOP TRADING OR REDUCE SIZE TO 0.25% FOR REST OF MONTH<br/>

**<Highlight variant="blue">Why this works:</Highlight>**

**<CheckCircle className="w-4 h-4 inline" stroke="#22c55e" />** Prevents catastrophic monthly drawdowns<br/>
**<CheckCircle className="w-4 h-4 inline" stroke="#22c55e" />** Signals that market conditions or your system may need adjustment<br/>
**<CheckCircle className="w-4 h-4 inline" stroke="#22c55e" />** Protects capital for next month's opportunities<br/>
**<CheckCircle className="w-4 h-4 inline" stroke="#22c55e" />** Prevents psychological breakdown<br/>

## MONTE CARLO SIMULATION: SEEING THE FUTURE

The most powerful tool for understanding drawdowns is Monte Carlo simulation.

### <Highlight variant='accent'>WHAT IS MONTE CARLO SIMULATION?</Highlight>

**<Highlight variant='blue'>Definition:</Highlight>** Running your trading system thousands of times with random trade sequences
to see the range of possible outcomes.

**<Highlight variant='blue'>Why it matters:</Highlight>** It shows you:

→ Your expected drawdown distribution<br/>
→ Worst-case scenarios<br/>
→ Probability of catastrophic drawdown<br/>
→ How long drawdowns typically last

### <Highlight variant='accent'>SIMPLE MONTE CARLO EXAMPLE</Highlight>

**<Highlight variant='blue'>Your system:</Highlight>**

→ Win rate: 50%<br/>
→ Average win: +2R<br/>
→ Average loss: -1R<br/>
→ Expectancy: +0.5R per trade<br/>
→ Monte Carlo runs 10,000 simulations of 500 trades each.

**<Highlight variant='blue'>Results:</Highlight>**

→ Median final equity: +250R (expected)<br/>
→ Best simulation: +420R<br/>
→ Worst simulation: +80R<br/>
→ Average drawdown: -15R (-15%)<br/>
→ 95th percentile drawdown: -35R (-35%)<br/>
→ Worst drawdown: -48R (-48%)<br/>

**<Highlight variant='blue'>Translation:</Highlight>**

Your system expects +250R over 500 trades (+250% at 1% risk).

**<Highlight variant='red'>BUT:</Highlight>**

**<Highlight variant='blue'>You have a 5% chance of experiencing a -35% drawdown.</Highlight>**
**<Highlight variant='blue'>You have a 1% chance of experiencing a -48% drawdown.</Highlight>**

**<Highlight variant='blue'>Can you survive -48%?</Highlight>**

If no, reduce position size to 0.5% per trade.<br/>
Now worst-case drawdown is -24% (survivable).

**<Highlight variant='red'>Monte Carlo doesn't predict the future — it shows you the RANGE of possible futures.</Highlight>**
