## WHAT IS RISK-REWARD RATIO? (THE DEFINITION THAT CHANGES EVERYTHING)

Let's start with the foundation — what does <Highlight variant="yellow">risk-reward ratio</Highlight> actually mean?

### <Highlight variant="accent">THE SIMPLE DEFINITION</Highlight>

<Math>
  {
    '\\text{Risk-Reward Ratio (RR)} = \\frac{\\text{Potential Profit}}{\\text{Potential Loss}}'
  }
</Math>
<br />

Or, more precisely:

<Math>
  {
    '\\text{RR} = \\frac{\\text{Target Price} - \\text{Entry Price}}{\\text{Entry Price} - \\text{Stop Loss Price}}'
  }
</Math>

**<Highlight variant="yellow">EXAMPLE 1: The 1:2 Risk-Reward Trade</Highlight>**

**Long EUR/USD:**

→ Entry: 1.1000<br/>
→ Stop loss: 1.0950 (50 pips below)<br/>
→ Target: 1.1100 (100 pips above)

**<Highlight variant="blue">Calculate RR:</Highlight>**

→ Risk = 50 pips<br/>
→ Reward = 100 pips<br/>
→ RR = 100 ÷ 50 = 2.0

**This is a 1:2 risk-reward ratio.**

For every $1 you risk, you're targeting $2 in profit.

If you risk $100 (your stop loss), you're targeting $200 profit.

Simple, right?

But here's where it gets interesting — and where most traders fail to grasp the <Highlight variant="blue">profound implications.</Highlight>

## THE PROFITABILITY EQUATION: WIN RATE × RISK-REWARD = YOUR FUTURE

Here's the mathematical truth that will determine whether you succeed or fail as a trader:

**Your profitability is determined by TWO variables:**

1\. <Highlight variant="yellow">Win Rate</Highlight> (% of trades that hit target)<br/>
2\. <Highlight variant="yellow">Risk-Reward Ratio</Highlight> (average reward ÷ average risk)<br/>

You <Highlight variant="green">can be profitable</Highlight> with:

→ High win rate + Low RR<br/>
→ Low win rate + High RR<br/>
→ Moderate win rate + Moderate RR<br/>

You <Highlight variant="red">CANNOT be profitable</Highlight> with:

→ Low win rate + Low RR<br/>

## THE BREAKEVEN WIN RATE: THE NUMBER YOU MUST KNOW

## HOW TO SET REALISTIC PROFIT TARGETS

Now that you understand optimal RR ratios, let's discuss <Highlight variant="blue">HOW to actually set your profit targets.</Highlight>

**<Highlight variant="blue">Rule #1:</Highlight>** Your target must be based on market structure, not arbitrary ratios.

**<Highlight variant="red">Wrong approach:</Highlight>**

→ "My stop is 50 pips, so my target is 100 pips (1:2 RR)."<br/>
→ You place target at 100 pips even though there's massive resistance at 80 pips.<br/>
→ Price hits 80 pips, reverses, hits your stop.<br/>
→ You turned a winning trade into a loser because you ignored structure.

**<Highlight variant="green">Right approach:</Highlight>**

→ "My stop is 50 pips. The next major resistance is at 120 pips.
That's 1:2.4 RR. I'll target 115 pips to stay below resistance."<br/>
→ Price hits your target at 115 pips before reaching resistance.<br/>
→ Winner.<br/>

**Your RR ratio is determined by market structure, not the other way around.**

### <Highlight variant="accent">METHOD #1: SUPPORT/RESISTANCE TARGETS</Highlight>

**<Highlight variant="blue">For longs:</Highlight>**

→ Target = Next major resistance level

**<Highlight variant="blue">For shorts:</Highlight>**

→ Target = Next major support level

**<Highlight variant="yellow">EXAMPLE:</Highlight>**

**<Highlight variant="blue">Long EUR/USD at 1.1000</Highlight>**<br/>

→ Stop: 1.0950 (50 pips below, at support breakdown)<br/>
→ Next resistance: 1.1120 (120 pips above)<br/>
→ Target: 1.1115 (120 pips, just below resistance)<br/>
→ RR: 120 ÷ 50 = 2.4

### <Highlight variant="accent">METHOD #2: ATR-BASED TARGETS</Highlight>

Use ATR to set targets that adapt to volatility (same principle as ATR stops).

**<Highlight variant="blue">Formula:</Highlight>**

<Math>
  {
    '\\text{Target} = \\text{Entry} \\pm (\\text{ATR} \\times \\text{Multiplier})'
  }
</Math>

**<Highlight variant="blue">Common multipliers:</Highlight>**

→ <Highlight variant="yellow">2× ATR:</Highlight> Conservative target (higher win rate)<br/>
→ <Highlight variant="yellow">3× ATR:</Highlight> Balanced target (moderate win rate)<br/>
→ <Highlight variant="yellow">4× ATR:</Highlight> Aggressive target (lower win rate)<br/>

**<Highlight variant="yellow">EXAMPLE:</Highlight>**

**<Highlight variant="blue">Long EUR/USD at 1.1000</Highlight>**<br/>

→ <Highlight variant="yellow">14-period ATR:</Highlight> 60 pips<br/>
→ <Highlight variant="yellow">Stop:</Highlight> 3× ATR = 180 pips below = 1.0820<br/>
→ <Highlight variant="yellow">Target:</Highlight> 3× ATR = 180 pips above = 1.1180<br/>
→ <Highlight variant="yellow">RR:</Highlight> 180 ÷ 180 = 1:1<br/>
→ Wait—that's only 1:1 RR!

**<Highlight variant="green">Solution:</Highlight>** Use asymmetric ATR multipliers:

→ <Highlight variant="yellow">Stop:</Highlight> 3× ATR = 180 pips below<br/>
→ <Highlight variant="yellow">Target:</Highlight> 6× ATR = 360 pips above = 1.1360<br/>
→ <Highlight variant="yellow">RR:</Highlight> 360 ÷ 180 = 2:1

### <Highlight variant="accent">METHOD #3: MEASURED MOVES</Highlight>

For chart patterns, use the pattern's height to project targets.

**<Highlight variant="yellow">EXAMPLE:</Highlight>** Ascending Triangle

**<Highlight variant="yellow">Pattern height:</Highlight>** 100 pips (from support to resistance)

**<Highlight variant="blue">Breakout:</Highlight>** 1.1000
**<Highlight variant="blue">Stop:</Highlight>** Below triangle support at 1.0950 (50 pips)
**<Highlight variant="blue">Target:</Highlight>** Breakout + Pattern height = 1.1000 + 100 = 1.1100

**<Highlight variant="yellow">RR:</Highlight>** 100 ÷ 50 = 2:1

### <Highlight variant="accent">METHOD #4: FIBONACCI EXTENSIONS</Highlight>

For trend trades, use Fibonacci extensions (1.272, 1.618, 2.618) as targets.

**<Highlight variant="yellow">EXAMPLE:</Highlight>**

**<Highlight variant="blue">Uptrend with pullback:</Highlight>**

→ Swing low: 1.0900<br/>
→ Swing high: 1.1100 (200 pips rally)<br/>
→ Pullback to: 1.1000 (50% retracement)

**<Highlight variant="blue">Entry:</Highlight>** 1.1000 (pullback bounce)
**<Highlight variant="blue">Stop:</Highlight>** 1.0950 (below pullback low)
**<Highlight variant="blue">Target:</Highlight>** 1.618 Fibonacci extension = 1.1224

**<Highlight variant="yellow">RR:</Highlight>** (1.1224 - 1.1000) ÷ (1.1000 - 1.0950) = 224 ÷ 50 = 4.48:1

### <Highlight variant="accent">METHOD #5: PARTIAL TARGETS (SCALING OUT)</Highlight>

Professional approach: Take partial profits at multiple levels.

**<Highlight variant="yellow">EXAMPLE:</Highlight>**

## Slippage, Costs, and Real-World Execution

Your theoretical RRR isn't your actual RRR once you account for market realities.

### <Highlight variant="accent">SLIPPAGE: THE HIDDEN TAX</Highlight>

Slippage is the <Highlight variant="yellow">difference</Highlight> between your <Highlight variant="yellow">intended price</Highlight> and your <Highlight variant="yellow">actual execution price.</Highlight>

**<Highlight variant="yellow">Example:</Highlight>**

**<Highlight variant="blue">Planned trade:</Highlight>**

→ <Highlight variant="yellow">Entry:</Highlight> $100<br/>
→ <Highlight variant="yellow">Stop:</Highlight> $95<br/>
→ <Highlight variant="yellow">Target:</Highlight> $110<br/>
→ <Highlight variant="yellow">Theoretical RRR:</Highlight> 2:1

**<Highlight variant="blue">Actual execution:</Highlight>**

→ <Highlight variant="yellow">Entry:</Highlight> $100.05 (slippage on entry)<br/>
→ <Highlight variant="yellow">Stop:</Highlight> $94.85 (slippage on stop)<br/>
→ <Highlight variant="yellow">Target:</Highlight> $109.90 (slippage on exit)<br/>

**<Highlight variant="blue">Actual trade:</Highlight>**

→ <Highlight variant="yellow">Risk:</Highlight> $100.05 - $94.85 = $5.20<br/>
→ <Highlight variant="yellow">Reward:</Highlight> $109.90 - $100.05 = $9.85<br/>
→ <Highlight variant="yellow">Actual RRR:</Highlight> 1.89:1<br/>

**Your 2:1 trade just became a 1.89:1 trade due to slippage.**

### <Highlight variant="accent">SLIPPAGE BY MARKET TYPE</Highlight>

**<Highlight variant="yellow">Highly Liquid (SPY, major forex):</Highlight>**

→ Slippage: 0.01-0.05%<br/>
→ <Highlight variant="yellow">Minimal impact on RRR</Highlight>

**Moderately Liquid (Most large-cap stocks):**

→ Slippage: 0.05-0.15%<br/>

**Low Liquidity (Small-caps, options):**

→ Slippage: 0.2-1%+<br/>
→ <Highlight variant="yellow">Significant impact on RRR</Highlight>

**Highly Illiquid (Penny stocks, exotic pairs):**

→ Slippage: 1-5%+<br/>
→ Devastating impact on RRR

### <Highlight variant="accent">COMMISSION AND SPREAD IMPACT</Highlight>

**<Highlight variant="yellow">Example: Forex Trade</Highlight>**

→ <Highlight variant="yellow">Spread:</Highlight> 2 pips ($20 on standard lot)<br/>

### <Highlight variant="accent">PRE-TRADE RR PROTOCOL</Highlight>

7\. <Highlight variant="blue">If</Highlight> RR &lt;1:1.5, don't take the trade (insufficient asymmetry)<br/>
8\. <Highlight variant="blue">Adjust</Highlight> position size based on your RR (higher RR = potentially larger size within risk limits)<br/>
9\. <Highlight variant="blue">Consider</Highlight> scaling out at multiple targets (1:1, 1:2, 1:3)<br/>
10\. <Highlight variant="blue">Account for</Highlight> spread/commission in your calculation (5-10% erosion)<br/>
11\. <Highlight variant="blue">Confirm</Highlight> this trade fits your style's optimal RR (scalp: 1:1-1.5, swing: 1:2-3)<br/>
12\. <Highlight variant="blue">Accept</Highlight> that this trade has X% chance of losing (expected, not personal)<br/>

### <Highlight variant="accent">DURING-TRADE RR PROTOCOL</Highlight>

1\. <Highlight variant="red">Do NOT</Highlight> move your target closer (let your RR play out)<br/>
2\. <Highlight variant="red">Do NOT</Highlight> exit early due to fear (trust your RR plan)<br/>
3\. <Highlight variant="green">DO</Highlight> consider trailing stop once 1:1 hit (protect profits)<br/>
4\. <Highlight variant="blue">Monitor</Highlight> price action at your target level (resistance might cause rejection)<br/>
5\. <Highlight variant="blue">If</Highlight> using scaling out, execute at your predetermined levels<br/>
6\. <Highlight variant="red">Do NOT</Highlight> move your target further away hoping for more (greed destroys RR discipline)

### <Highlight variant="accent">POST-TRADE RR ANALYSIS</Highlight>

1\. <Highlight variant="blue">Record</Highlight> actual RR achieved (not just planned RR)<br/>
2\. <Highlight variant="blue">Note</Highlight> execution costs (spread, slippage, commission)<br/>
3\. <Highlight variant="blue">Compare</Highlight> planned vs. actual target (did market structure validate your target?)<br/>
4\. <Highlight variant="blue">Assess</Highlight>: Was my RR realistic for this setup? (learn and adjust)<br/>
5\. <Highlight variant="blue">Track</Highlight> your average RR over 20+ trades (see your true RR distribution)<br/>
6\. <Highlight variant="blue">Calculate</Highlight> your expectancy (more on this in Lesson 2.5)
