One side gains the upper hand, price moves in their direction, then balance returns.
This is the heartbeat of price movement — the reason charts look the way they do.

## The Constant Push and Pull

Buyers are always pushing upward, trying to lift price higher.
Sellers are always pushing downward, trying to drive it lower.

When these forces balance, price stabilizes.
But the moment one side grows stronger — through conviction, volume, or new participants —
the balance breaks, and price shifts.

Price doesn’t move randomly.
It moves toward the side that wins the battle, then pauses once balance is restored.
And that rhythm — push, pull, equilibrium — repeats endlessly.

### <Highlight variant="accent">See It Live in 3D</Highlight>

Now, let's watch it unfold.

At first, the middle zone (the spread) barely moves.
Collisions flash inside, but buyers and sellers are evenly matched — equilibrium.

Now use the controls to give either side the upper hand.
Watch what happens.

### When Buyers Gain Control

Slide the control toward Buy Pressure.
Green particles surge with activity.
The spread rises. The price marker climbs.

Buyers are lifting price, absorbing selling pressure until they lose momentum or attract sellers who see new value at higher prices.
Then — balance again, at a higher level.

### When Sellers Take Over

Slide toward Sell Pressure.
Red particles dominate.
The spread drops. The price marker falls.

Sellers overwhelm buyers, pushing price lower.
Eventually, at some level, buyers see opportunity and step back in — and balance returns.

### Back to Neutral

Bring the control to Neutral or Equilibrium.
Watch how the system calms.
The glow fades. Particles slow.
The market catches its breath before the next battle.

## What You’re Really Seeing

This isn’t just an animation — it’s the mechanism behind every price move on a chart.

Every candle. Every spike. Every consolidation.
They all come from this same battle playing out at different time scales.

→ A strong <Highlight variant="green">green candle</Highlight> = <Highlight variant="green">buyers</Highlight> winning decisively.<br/>
→ A sharp <Highlight variant="red">red candle</Highlight> = <Highlight variant="red">sellers</Highlight> taking control.<br/>
→ A sideways range = <Highlight variant="yellow">equilibrium</Highlight> — a stalemate between forces.

## Why This Matters for Trading

Understanding this battle changes how you read charts.
You stop seeing randomness and start recognizing the underlying force dynamics:

→ <Highlight variant="yellow">Is one side exhausted?</Highlight><br/>
If buyers push price up but volume fades, they might be running out of energy.<br/>
→ <Highlight variant="yellow">Are sidelines waiting to join?</Highlight><br/>
If gray particles cluster near one zone, pressure is building for a breakout.<br/>
→ <Highlight variant="yellow">Where will the next equilibrium form?</Highlight><br/>
Likely near previous battle zones — old support and resistance areas.<br/>

The market is a continuous auction for fairness, always searching for the next fair price through this push and pull.
Your edge as a trader comes from reading the balance of power — before it becomes obvious.
