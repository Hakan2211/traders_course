## The Trader Who Was Fighting Himself

His name was <Highlight variant="blue">Gregor</Highlight>. High-energy, fast-thinking, stimulation-seeking personality.
The kind of guy who talked fast, moved fast, thought fast.
Always had three conversations going simultaneously. <Highlight variant="blue">Thrived in chaos. Got bored easily.</Highlight><br/>

He'd been trying to become a swing trader for two years.<br/>
His mentor — a successful swing trader with a calm, patient, methodical approach — had told him:
_<span style={{color: '#facc15'}}>"Swing trading is superior. You hold positions for days or weeks. You're not glued to the screen. You let the big moves develop.
It's less stressful, more profitable, more sustainable."</span>_

So Gregor tried.<br/>
He'd enter a swing trade based on solid analysis. Target was 5-10 days away. Stop was wide enough for normal volatility. Everything by the book.

**<Highlight variant="blue">Day 1:</Highlight>** Position slightly green. Gregor checked it 47 times. Felt restless.
Read every piece of news about the stock. Couldn't focus on anything else.<br/>

**<Highlight variant="blue">Day 2:</Highlight>** Position up 2%. Gregor felt the urge to take profit.
_<span style={{color: '#f7ef8a'}}>"No, no," he told himself. "This is a swing trade. Let it develop."</span>_<br/>
He closed his platform to avoid the temptation. Opened it again 10 minutes later.<br/>

**<Highlight variant="blue">Day 3:</Highlight>** Position up 3%, then gave back 1%. Gregor couldn't stand it. The intraday volatility was torture.
His heart rate was elevated all day. He couldn't sleep that night thinking about the position.<br/>

**<Highlight variant="blue">Day 4, 10:00 AM:</Highlight>** Gregor closed the trade at +2.5%.
_<span style={{color: '#f7ef8a'}}>"I can't take this anymore. I'll find another swing trade."</span>_<br/>

The stock went on to hit his original target — a +8% move — four days later.<br/>
This pattern repeated for two years.

Gregor would analyze perfect swing setups. Enter with conviction.
Then torture himself for days, checking obsessively, unable to focus, experiencing physical stress symptoms, and eventually exiting way too early.<br/>
His mentor kept telling him:<br/>
_<span style={{color: '#facc15'}}>"You need more discipline. You need to be patient. You need to trust your analysis."</span>_<br/>

But it wasn't working. Gregor felt like a failure.<br/>
_<span style={{color: '#f7ef8a'}}>"Everyone says swing trading is better. Why can't I do it? What's wrong with me?"</span>_<br/>
